# 🩺 Impact of Handwashing on Maternal Mortality: A Data Analysis Study

## Project Overview

This project analyzes the historical work of Dr. Ignaz Semmelweis, who investigated the high maternal mortality rates in maternity clinics during the 1840s. Using data analysis techniques in Python, this study examines the relationship between handwashing practices and maternal deaths.

The project demonstrates how data-driven decision-making and public health interventions can significantly improve patient outcomes.

---

## Objectives

* Analyze maternal mortality trends across different clinics.
* Compare mortality rates before and after the introduction of handwashing.
* Calculate the proportion of deaths relative to births.
* Evaluate the effectiveness of hand hygiene practices.
* Generate data-driven insights from historical healthcare data.

---

## Datasets Used

### 1. Yearly Deaths by Clinic

Contains yearly records of births and maternal deaths across two clinics.

### 2. Monthly Deaths

Contains monthly birth and death records used to evaluate the impact of handwashing practices over time.

## Key Findings

* Clinic 1 recorded higher maternal mortality rates than Clinic 2.
* Significant differences existed between clinic outcomes.
* Maternal mortality decreased substantially after handwashing was introduced.
* Hand hygiene practices had a measurable positive impact on patient survival.
* Data strongly supports Dr. Semmelweis's hypothesis regarding infection control.
